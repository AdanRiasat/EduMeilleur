# Introduction to Vectors Exercises

## Exercise 1: Identifying Vectors

Determine whether each object below can be represented by a vector.

- a) Temperature outside
- b) Wind speed and direction
- c) Velocity of a car
- d) Distance between two cities

---

## Exercise 2: Magnitude or Direction

For each statement, identify whether it describes the **magnitude** or the **direction** of a vector.

- a) The vector points 45° above the x-axis.
- b) The vector has a length of 12 units.
- c) The arrow points south-west.
- d) The vector is 7 cm long.

---

## Exercise 3: Vector Notation

Rewrite each vector using the requested notation.

- a) Write vector AB using arrow notation above the letters.
- b) Write vector v using vector notation.
- c) Identify the initial and terminal points of vector CD.

---

## Exercise 4: Equal Vectors

Determine whether the following pairs of vectors are equal.

- a) Two vectors both have magnitude 5 and direction 90°.
- b) One vector has magnitude 8 and direction 45°, while the other has magnitude 8 and direction 225°.
- c) Two vectors have the same direction but different magnitudes.

---

## Exercise 5: Parallel or Anti-Parallel

State whether the vectors are:

- Parallel
- Anti-parallel
- Neither

- a) Vector A points east and Vector B points east.
- b) Vector C points north and Vector D points south.
- c) Vector E points 30° and Vector F points 120°.

---

## Exercise 6: Magnitude of a Vector

A vector has an initial point at A(2, 1) and a terminal point at B(8, 5).

- a) Find the horizontal change.
- b) Find the vertical change.
- c) Use the distance formula to find the magnitude of the vector.

---

## Exercise 7: Direction of a Vector

A vector starts at the origin and ends at the point (4, 4).

- a) Sketch the vector.
- b) Estimate its direction angle θ with respect to the positive x-axis.

---

## Exercise 8: Real-World Vectors

A boat travels 15 km east.

- a) What is the magnitude of the vector?
- b) What is the direction of the vector?
- c) Represent the movement as a vector.

# ✅ Answers

## Exercise 1

- a) No — temperature has magnitude only.
- b) Yes — wind has magnitude and direction.
- c) Yes — velocity has magnitude and direction.
- d) No — distance has magnitude only.

## Exercise 2

- a) Direction
- b) Magnitude
- c) Direction
- d) Magnitude

## Exercise 3

- b) \( \vec{v} \)
- c) Initial point = C, terminal point = D

## Exercise 4

- a) Yes — same magnitude and direction.
- b) No — directions are opposite.
- c) No — magnitudes are different.

- b) Anti-parallel

## Exercise 6

- a) Horizontal change = 8 - 2 = 6
- b) Vertical change = 5 - 1 = 4
- c) Magnitude:

\[
||\vec{v}|| = \sqrt{6^2 + 4^2}
= \sqrt{36 + 16}
= \sqrt{52}
\approx 7.21
\]

- a) Vector goes diagonally upward to the right.
- b) Direction angle:

\]

- b) Direction = East
- c) Example representation:

\[

\]
