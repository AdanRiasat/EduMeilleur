# ➡️ Vectors and Components Exercises

## Exercise 1: Identifying Vector Information

For each situation below, determine whether the information describes:

- A vector
- A scalar
- The magnitude of a vector
- The direction of a vector

- a) A plane travels 500 km north.
- b) A temperature of 25°C.
- c) A vector has a length of 12 units.
- d) A vector points at an angle of 120°.

## Exercise 2: Vector Components from Coordinates

Find the components of each vector.

- a) A(1, 2) → B(6, 5)
- b) C(-2, 4) → D(3, -1)
- c) E(0, 0) → F(7, 9)

Use:

\[
\overrightarrow{AB} = (x_2 - x_1,\ y_2 - y_1)
\]

---

## Exercise 3: Magnitude of a Vector

Find the magnitude of each vector.

- a) \( \vec{v} = (3,4) \)
- b) \( \vec{u} = (5,12) \)
- c) \( \vec{w} = (-8,6) \)

Use:

\[
||\vec{v}|| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}
\]

## Exercise 5: Components from Magnitude and Direction

Find the approximate components of each vector.

Use:

\[
a = ||\vec{v}||\cos\theta
\]

\[
b = ||\vec{v}||\sin\theta
\]

- a) Magnitude = 10, direction = 30°
- b) Magnitude = 20, direction = 45°
- c) Magnitude = 15, direction = 120°

Round to the nearest tenth.

## Exercise 6: Finding Direction

Find the direction angle θ for each vector.

- a) \( (3,3) \)
- b) \( (5,2) \)
- c) \( (-4,4) \)

Round to the nearest degree.

## Exercise 3

### a)

\[
||\vec{v}|| = \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2}
= \sqrt{9+16}
= \sqrt{25}
= 5
\]

### b)

\[
||\vec{u}|| = \sqrt{5^2 + 12^2}
= \sqrt{25+144}
= \sqrt{169}
= 13
\]

### c)

\[
||\vec{w}|| = \sqrt{(-8)^2 + 6^2}
= \sqrt{64+36}
= \sqrt{100}
= 10
\]

\[
